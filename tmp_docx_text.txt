
Background Report: Mindy McIntyre
Chief Deputy Director, CalRecycle (California Department of Resources Recycling and Recovery)
Quick Facts & Professional Career
Role: Chief Deputy Director, CalRecycle (since Feb 2022). Former Deputy Cabinet Secretary in the Governor’s Office; earlier led Legislative Affairs at CalRecycle; prior roles in the State Senate and Planning & Conservation League. Governor of California+1
Public voice on circularity: Regular update lead at CalRecycle monthly meetings (SB 54 rulemaking, needs assessment, grants, advisory board).
International engagement: Represented California at plastics-treaty talks (INC-5) with Director Zoe Heller. linkedin.com
Podcast/briefings: Featured in Reloop’s “California’s Path to Zero Waste” on policy + standardized definitions + industry partnerships. Reloop+2Apple Podcasts+2
Current Priorities (as signaled publicly)
SB 54 implementation at pace: Managing comments, re-initiating regs, running needs assessment, and staffing the Packaging Producer Responsibility Advisory Board.
Beverage container system upgrades: Expanding redemption access via multi-cycle grants ($127M) and program tweaks.
Stewardship/EPR expansion beyond packaging: Pharmaceuticals, sharps, carpet, batteries, signal of a platform view across materials. CalRecycle
Disaster debris + permitting cadence: Operational competence alongside policy (permits, waivers, cleanups).
How She Works (useful for stakeholder strategy)
Data-first, comment-driven: Emphasizes formal comment review, needs assessment portals, listserv transparency.
Iterative regulation: Acknowledges timelines, commits to “rates and dates” set in statute, and signals updated timelines when needed.
Pragmatic on incentives: On PET quality-incentive payments (QIP), she publicly noted low uptake and the need to “recalibrate,” inviting stakeholder fixes. Resource-Recycling
Producer responsibility framing: Publicly reinforces that EPR works when producers design for reuse/recycling and fund outcomes. Instagram
Strengths & Risks (for Diversys mapping)
Strengths / Levers
Cross-material EPR perspective (packaging, carpet, pharma/sharps, batteries).
Operational + policy blend (permits, grants, disaster ops + SB 54 policy).
Openness to stakeholder input and iterative improvement (QIP, SB 54 needs assessment). Resource-Recycling
Constraints / Sensitivities
Budget and grant timing pressures; high public scrutiny on timelines.
Statutory guardrails (e.g., capped incentive levels) limit unilateral program changes. Resource-Recycling
Need for certainty for businesses amid regulatory shifts—clear guidance and reporting are valued.
What Will Likely Matter to Mindy in a Diversys Pitch
Execution support for SB 54: Tools that speed responses to advisory board, needs assessment, PRO reporting, and “rates & dates” progress tracking.
Clean, auditable data pipelines: Producer-grade validations; reconciliation across CalRecycle programs and adjacent agencies; easy public-facing summaries.
Incentive-aware design: Analytics that show how program levers (like QIPs, commingled rates) affect bale quality and compliance, evidence to “recalibrate” policy. Resource-Recycling
Scalable stewardship templates: Ready-to-run modules for batteries, carpet, pharma/sharps, so one platform supports many EPR programs.
Talk Tracks (what to say)
“We turn SB 54 obligations into transparent, producer-grade reporting and community-readable dashboards, reducing back-and-forth and accelerating guidance.”
“Our analytics help test incentive scenarios (e.g., PET QIP vs. commingled dynamics) so CalRecycle can calibrate policies with evidence.” Resource-Recycling
“Diversys is a single platform for multiple stewardship programs, packaging today, batteries/sharps/carpet tomorrow, lowering admin burden while improving data quality.”
Proof to Bring
Pilot plan (90–120 days): one packaging/EPR pilot + one stewardship pilot with defined KPIs (submission accuracy %, exception cycle time, bale-quality uplift).
Policy-ready outputs: Advisory-board packets, needs-assessment inputs, listserv-friendly summaries, and public-facing pages autogenerated from the same dataset.
Grant alignment: Show how platform reporting satisfies RIG and program reporting requirements.
What Not to Say (landmines)
❌ “We’ll enforce producer compliance.”Why: CalRecycle is a regulator, but Mindy stresses collaborative rulemaking and stakeholder input. Say: “We enable shared accountability with auditable data.”
❌ “Your incentives are broken; we’ll fix them.”Why: Incentives are partly statutory and politically sensitive. Say: “We model policy options and quantify impacts so you can tune programs.” Resource-Recycling
❌ “We’ll automate everything end-to-end.”Why: Overpromising undercuts trust; staff capacity and public engagement still matter. Say: “We reduce administrative drag, so teams focus on guidance, outreach, and permits.”
❌ “This is just a recycling problem.”Why: CalRecycle is advancing EPR across materials and broader circularity. Say: “One platform for multi-material stewardship and circular-economy KPIs.”
