assistant: Here is a practical approach I recommend when scope is unknown. It keeps the deal moving without giving away unlimited work.

## Recommended Path
Use a two stage commercial model.

### Stage 1: Paid Discovery or Scoping
Purpose: de risk unknowns and create a scoped plan.
- Fixed price or time boxed retainer
- Clear deliverables: requirements, solution design, project plan, estimate range, risks, assumptions
- Credit some or all of the fee toward implementation if they move forward

### Stage 2: Implement with a Range and Guardrails
Offer a range tied to complexity bands.
- Example: Small, Medium, Large tiers based on known drivers
- Provide a not to exceed range until discovery completes
- Convert to a firm scope and price after discovery

## What To Anchor On Before You Quote Anything
Minimum viable inputs for a high level price:
- Business objectives and success criteria
- Number of sites, users, and workflows
- Integrations and data migration needs
- Regulatory or compliance requirements
- Timeline constraints
- Internal client resources available

If they cannot provide this, you should not give a fixed number.

## How To Present It In The Deal
Suggested positioning:
- We can provide a reasonable range today, or a firm price after a short discovery engagement
- The range includes assumptions and excludes unknowns
- Any change to assumptions triggers a scope change and re estimate

## Guardrails That Protect You
- Document assumptions up front
- Define out of scope clearly
- Tie price to drivers, not vague effort
- Keep SOW modular so you can add or remove modules without restarting the contract

## Example High Level Language
- We price implementation based on scale and complexity, so without discovery we provide a range with explicit assumptions
- If you want fixed price, we start with a short paid discovery and convert to a firm quote
