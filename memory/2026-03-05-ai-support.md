# AI Support Agent Strategy Report for Diversys

## Executive Summary
Diversys can deploy an AI Support Agent to extend coverage outside business hours, improve response speed for international clients, and scale support without immediate headcount growth. Given low current ticket volume, the primary value is readiness for growth, 24x5 or 24x7 coverage, faster first response, and improved consistency of answers. The AI agent should operate with human escalation and strict guardrails to protect client trust and data privacy.

Recommended approach: a 3‑phase rollout over 8 months with a controlled pilot, then scale. Focus on Zendesk and chat first, then deeper Jira integration.

---

## Company Context and Fit
Diversys provides recycling management software with compliance, reporting, and data integrity at the core. The company already highlights AI capabilities in its product positioning. An AI Support Agent is aligned with that posture and supports a modern customer experience standard, while creating operational leverage as volume grows.

Public positioning signals:
- Operational data accuracy and compliance  
- High‑trust ecosystems  
- Growth and global reach  

This makes support quality and response speed strategic, not just operational.

Source: https://www.diversys.com/

---

## Why Now
- Customer expectations for instant or near‑instant response are growing  
- Time zone coverage is a competitive differentiator  
- Low volume today means you can pilot safely and build institutional knowledge before growth accelerates  
- AI tooling maturity has improved for support workflows  

---

## Goals for the AI Support Agent
Primary goals:
- After hours coverage from 5:00 p.m. to 9:00 a.m.  
- Faster first response and triage  
- Scale support without adding headcount immediately  
- Consistent knowledge sharing and reduced rework  

Secondary goals:
- Collect and standardize product feedback and issue patterns  
- Improve knowledge base quality  
- Reduce escalation delays  

---

## What It Is
An AI Support Agent is a controlled, multi‑channel assistant that:
- Answers common support questions  
- Triage and classify tickets  
- Retrieves relevant knowledge base content  
- Escalates complex or sensitive issues to a human  
- Operates under strict guardrails and logging  

---

## How It Works in Diversys
### Channels
- Zendesk for email tickets  
- Chat channel integration  
- Jira for escalation and engineering handoff  

### Typical flow
1) Incoming ticket or chat  
2) AI agent attempts answer using knowledge base and approved sources  
3) If confidence is high, reply with guidance and resolution steps  
4) If confidence is low, tag and escalate to human  
5) Create or link a Jira issue when needed  
6) Log all interactions for QA  

---

## Benefits

### 1) Scale without immediate headcount growth
- With low ticket volume now, the agent offers readiness for growth without delay  
- It prevents a support backlog as ticket volume increases  

### 2) Faster response and improved coverage
- Immediate first response after hours  
- Improved client satisfaction for international time zones  

### 3) Consistency and quality
- Standardized answers  
- Reduced variance across shifts and new hires  

### 4) Better knowledge management
- Captures recurring topics and flags gaps  
- Converts tribal knowledge into reusable assets  

---

## Security and Trust Considerations
This is essential for Diversys.

Security approach:
- Use data‑minimized prompts  
- Do not send client data to external LLM without contractual assurance  
- Restrict AI to read only from approved knowledge base content  
- Log and audit all AI responses  
- Human‑in‑the‑loop for sensitive categories  

Risk controls:
- Confidence thresholds for auto response  
- Escalation when low confidence or sensitive keywords appear  
- Admin approval for new knowledge entries  

---

## Cost Considerations and Ranges
These are directional ranges. Actual costs depend on volume, model selection, and tooling stack.

### 1) Platform and tooling
- AI orchestration platform: $3,000 to $25,000 per year  
- Knowledge base and connectors: $1,000 to $10,000 per year  
- Zendesk and Jira integration: low to moderate integration cost  

### 2) LLM usage
- At low volume, likely under $200 to $500 per month  
- If volume grows, costs may increase to $1,000 to $3,000 per month  

### 3) Implementation and support
- Initial implementation: $15,000 to $60,000  
- Ongoing tuning and governance: $1,000 to $5,000 per month  

---

## Comparison to Hiring
Hiring one additional support resource:
- Fully loaded cost often $80,000 to $120,000 per year  
- AI agent can operate 24x5 or 24x7 for a fraction of that cost  
- AI does not replace human support but reduces pressure on hiring timelines  

---

## Timing and Roadmap
Target: 8 months total, with clear go or no‑go gates.

### Phase 1: Discovery and Design, 4 to 6 weeks
- Define support use cases and escalation rules  
- Audit knowledge base  
- Establish data handling and security policy  

### Phase 2: Pilot, 8 to 10 weeks
- Limited scope, after hours only  
- Zendesk and chat integration  
- Measure response speed, deflection rate, and QA  

### Phase 3: Scale, 12 to 16 weeks
- Expand coverage  
- Add Jira escalation automation  
- Improve knowledge base coverage  

---

## KPIs for Board Oversight
- First response time  
- After hours response coverage rate  
- Deflection rate  
- Resolution time  
- Customer satisfaction  
- Escalation accuracy  
- Knowledge base coverage and freshness  

---

## Risks and Mitigations
### 1) Wrong answers  
Mitigation: confidence thresholds, limited domain, strict knowledge sources  

### 2) Security exposure  
Mitigation: data minimization, no sensitive data in prompts, audit logs  

### 3) Low adoption  
Mitigation: transparent customer messaging, clear escalation path, internal training  

---

## Strategic Recommendation
Proceed with a phased AI Support Agent rollout. This creates scalable support coverage without immediate headcount growth and positions Diversys as an innovation‑forward partner.

---

## Decision Request for the Board
Approval to proceed with a phased pilot, with a defined investment range and a go or no‑go gate after the pilot.

---

1) Confirm desired coverage level and KPI targets  
2) Select AI platform and integration approach  
3) Begin discovery and knowledge base audit  
4) Launch pilot and report metrics to leadership
